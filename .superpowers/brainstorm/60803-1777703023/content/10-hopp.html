What Hopp gets right

Photo = hero, not avatar. No circle crop, no ring, no frame. The trainer's physical presence fills the screen. This is the product. Everything else supports it.

Gradient IS the brand atmosphere. Hopp uses the profile subject's energy colour. We use warm terracotta — already in our palette, already distinctive. No stock background needed.

Two structural interpretations:

  • A — Atmospheric Hero: Photo fills the top 420px, name overlaid at the bottom as the gradient fades to content. Feels more native iOS, more dramatic. Better for scrolling depth.
  • B — Hopp Exact: Name first (above fold), then full-width photo, then white content. Cleaner information hierarchy — you know who before you see them. Closer to what Hopp ships.

The arcs and circles are gone. When the photo IS the atmosphere, you don't need decorative elements. The gradient does the work.

Why this is better than the circle: Real trainers have great photos. A circle crop at 80px shows nothing. A 400px full-bleed shows everything that matters — posture, confidence, energy, environment. This is what clients are actually buying.
